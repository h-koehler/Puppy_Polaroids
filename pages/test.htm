LABRADOR RETRIEVER

Origin

The Labrador Retriever originated from Newfoundlound and was bred as a sporting and hunting dog. As a sporting/hunting dog, it retrieved game and waterfowl on land and water, and served as a companion for hunters. The breed dates back to the 1830s, where St. John's water dogs (bred by European settlers in Newfoundland) were bred with British hunting dogs to create the Labrador.

Characteristics

The Labrador is a medium-large breed that ranges from 55-80 lbs in weight and 21.1-24.5 in in height. The Lab's coat can be three colors: black, yellow, and chocolate. As a gun dog specifically bred for water retrieving, the Labrador has fully webbed paws, an otter-like tail, and a water-resistant double coat (which helps the dog stay warm when in the water in the winter). These traits combined make the Lab an innate strong swimmer. The Lab also has a soft mouth, which allows it to carry game and waterfowl without damaging it.

The Labrador's personality is friendly, energetic, and playful. They bond well with their family and are sociable with neighbors. They are a high-energy breed and require lots of exercise to keep them mentally and physically fit. The Lab is also highly intelligent, has the ability to take initiative, and self direction, making it an excellent service dog.

Today

Today the Lab is considered the most popular breed in the world, and is the most popular dog by ownership in Canada, New Zealand, ad the UK. They have also been the number one dog in the US for 31 years. This is because, in addition to being a great family dog, the Lab's disposition makes it an excellent service and therapy dog. In fact, Labs are the most popular breed of assistance dog in the US and Australia and have the highest success rate in competing guide dog school. Approximately 60-70% of all guide dogs in the US are Labradors. The Labrador is also widely used by police and other officials for their detection and working abilities, such as drug and explosive detection, water rescue, and search and rescue.

BERNESE MOUNTAIN DOG

Origin

The Bernese Mountain Dog originated from Switzerland and the Swiss Alps. Their German name Berner Sennenhund is derived from Senne which means “alpine pasture” and Hund (hound/dog). They are one of four breeds of Sennenhund. The other three are: Greater Swiss Mountain Dog, Appenzeller Mountain Dog, Entlebucher Mountain Dog. The Bernese Mountain Dog is a descendant of working dogs taken to Switzerland by invading Romans more than 2,000 years ago.

As a working dog, these dogs were used as all purpose farm dogs. They were used to protect farms from predators, drive cattle to and from their pastures, transport carts of milk and cheese (earning the title “Cheese Dogs”), and as companions to their handlers.

Characteristics

The Bernese Mountain Dog is a large, heavy dog that ranges from 75–120 lbs in weight and 23-27.5 in in height. It has a double coat; a short undercoat and a long, silky outcoat. The outer coat is tri-colored; it is black with a white chest and rust-colored markings on the front of legs, sides of mouth and chest, and above the eyes. Because of their coat, these dogs shed regularly. The Bernese Mountain Dog is also considered a dry-mouthed breed, which means it produces less drool than other big dogs.

The Bernese Mountain Dog is good-natured, self-assured, and affectionate. They are patient and docile dogs that are gentle with children. These dogs are eager to please and are devoted to their family, so they do not do well when left alone for too long. These dogs also need regular activity and exercise to stay mentally and physically fit.

Today

Today the Bernese Mountain Dog is one of the most popular dog breeds in the US, ranking at #22 in 2022.

SHIBA INU

Origin

The Shiba Inu is an ancient dog that originated in Japan in 300 BC. The Shiba was bred to hunt and flush out small game, such as birds and rabbits, in the mountainous regions of Japan. This is also where its name is derived from; “shiba” means brushwood in Japanese, and “inu” means dog.

After World War II, the Shiba almost became extinct due to food shortage and a post-war distemper epidemic. Because of this, today’s Shiba Inu is a combination of the three surviving bloodlines: the Shinshu Shiba from Nagano Prefecture, the Mino Shiba from the former Mino Province, and the San’in Shiba from Tottori and Shimane Prefectures.

Characteristics

The Shiba Inu is a small, muscular dog that ranges from 17-23 lbs in weight and 13.5-16.5 in in height. It has a thick double coat and a bushy tail that curls over its back. The outer coat is stiff and straight and the undercoat is soft and dense. Their coat comes in four colors, red, sesame, black and tan, and cream. Most also have markings known as “urajiro” (underside white).

From the Japanese breed standard, the Shiba Inu has spirited boldness, good nature, and a feeling of artlessness. The Shiba can do well with other dogs, kids, but, as with all dogs, requires adequate socialization from a young age. Shiba’s are highly intelligent and can be very headstrong. Training can be difficult and requires a patient and consistent handler. The Shiba can be a vocal dog, and has a distinguishing “shiba scream”.

Today

Today the Shiba In is the number one companion dog in Japan, and is growing in popularity in the United States (currently ranked 43rd). The Shiba Inu has had a significant presence in online culture since at least 2010. For example, the creation of Doge, an Internet meme from 2013, and the cryptocurrency Dogecoin, which is named after the meme.

GOLDEN RETRIEVER

Origin

The Golden Retriever is a sporting/gun dog that originated from Scotland in the 19th century. The breed was created by Sir Dudley Majoribanks, who crossbred Flat-coated Retrievers with Tweed Water Spaniels, and some other British dog breeds. Majoribank’s goal was to create a dog that could retrieve birds either in water or land and from far distances. The Golden Retriever also served as a companion dog to their owners and families.

Characteristics

The Golden Retriever is a strong, medium-sized dog that ranges from 20-24 in in height and 55-75 lbs in weight. It has a thick double coat that has a medium length outer coat that can be flat or wavy, and a dense undercoat that provides weather resistance. The coat can vary greatly, from light cream to mahogany. Because of its origin, the Golden Retriever is a water-loving dog and is a strong swimmer.

The Golden Retriever is intelligent, gentle, playful, and affectionate. Goldens are known for being an excellent family pet, as they are generally extremely tolerant of children and are devoted to their owners. They are also eager to please and are among the most obedient dog breeds, consistently winning in standardized obedience, agility, and field trials. However, they do require a patient and consistent trainer and a fenced-in yard during off-leash exercise, as their curiosity can cause them to be easily distracted. They also require regular mental stimulation and challenges in order to maintain mental and physical fitness.

Today

Today the Golden Retriever is one of the most popular companion dogs in the UK, US, Australia, and Canada. In the US, it ranked 3rd in popularity and is regularly in the top 5. The Golden Retriever is a regular competitor at dog shows, can be trained as guide dogs and therapy dogs, and may compete in obedience trials and other dog sports.
