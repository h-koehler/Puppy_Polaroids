LABRADOR RETRIEVER

Origin

The Labrador Retriever originated from Newfoundlound and was bred as a sporting and hunting dog. As a sporting/hunting dog, it retrived game and waterfowl on land and water, and served as a companion for hunters. The breed dates back to the 1830s, where St. John's water dogs (bred by European settlers in Newfoundlound) were bred with British hunting dogs to create the Labrador.

Characteristics

The Labrador is a medium-large breed that ranges from 55-80 lbs in weight and 21.1-24.5 in in height. The Lab's coat can be three colors: black, yellow, and chocolate. As a gun dog specifically bred for water retrieving, the Labrador has fully webbed paws, an otter-like tail, and a water-resistant double coat (which helps the dog stay warm when in the water in the winter). These traits combined make the Lab an inately strong swimmer. The Lab also has a soft mouth, which allows it to carry game and waterfowl without damaging it.

The Labrador's personality is friendly, energetic, and playful. They bond well with their family and are socialable with neighbors. They are a high-energy breed and require lots of exercise to keep them mentally and physically fit. The Lab is also highly intelligent, has the ability to take initiative, and self direction, making it an excellent service dog.

Today

Today the Lab is considered the most popular breed in the world, and is the most popular dog by ownership in Canada, New Zealand, ad the UK. They have also been the number one dog in the US for 31 years. This is because, in addition to being a great family dog, the Lab's disposition makes it an excellent service dog. In fact, Labs are the most popular breed of assistaance dog in the US and Australia. Approsimately 60-70% of all guide dogs in the US are Labradors. This makes sense seeing as Labs have the highest success rate in completing guide dog school. The Labrador is also widely used by police and other officials for their detection and working abilities.
